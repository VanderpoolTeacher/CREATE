# Durable Skills Framework

Students are remarkably good at the game we designed for them: hand them a list of boxes, they check every one. But college and the workforce offer no boxes — students are handed an outcome and expected to figure out the rest. These six skill clusters are what that shift actually requires.

---

## The Six Skill Clusters

### 1. Decomposition
**What it is:** Taking a big, ambiguous goal and breaking it into ordered, doable steps — without being handed a checklist first.
**Looks like in a student:** Can follow a checklist flawlessly but stalls when given a multi-step project to organize on their own.

### 2. Work & Time Organization
**What it is:** Managing tasks and deadlines across multiple concurrent projects; using a calendar to define and protect work time; keeping files organized so anything can be found again.
**Looks like in a student:** Misses that two project deadlines collide; can't find where their own files are saved.

### 3. Working with Ambiguity
**What it is:** Asking clarifying questions early in a project and adapting when requirements shift as more becomes known.
**Looks like in a student:** Goes silent when a project is introduced, then sends a flood of questions the night before it's due.

### 4. Initiative / Self-Starting
**What it is:** Getting going without being prompted; investigating a problem independently and proposing a next step before stopping or waiting on someone else. This is often the hardest shift.
**Looks like in a student:** Waits to be told the next step instead of making a move.

### 5. Communication & Presentation
**What it is:** Speaking about your own work in front of a group — explaining what you did, why, and what you learned.
**Looks like in a student:** Little practice standing up to present; even working from note cards can feel unfamiliar.

### 6. Digital & Systems Fluency
**What it is:** Comfort with how software actually works and where information lives — understanding abstraction at a basic level. This skill is increasingly urgent as AI tools become part of everyday work.
**Looks like in a student:** Doesn't know where files are stored on their own computer.

---

*These clusters are meant to be kind to everyone in the room. Students aren't broken — they mastered the game we gave them. This framework names what the next game asks for.*
